# The Great Filter 
- I recently watched a great Youtube video by Kurzgesagt about the so called 'great filter'. 
- It is always highly intriguing to learn something that really does affect us on an evolutionary scale for a change. The Great Filter is an idea
proposed as an aide to understand our place in the universe(amongst possibly other civilizations). It proposes that since there are potentially millions of earthlike planets 
in our galaxy alone, and even travelling at a fraction of the speed of light, the entire galaxy can be colonized in about 100 millions years, the reason why we have not witnessed other worldy organisms is because: They go extinct before they are 
able to make the giant leap(they are not able to pass through the filter).
- They can be many reasons as to why intelligent species are not able to pass through that ultimate barrier. 
- Take our species for example, we have evolved to a stage where we have left our resource-hungry and competitive species behaviour in the past 
or we like to think. But the reality is that we are still descendents of the same apes that would kill for food or land. It is part of the reason how we came to become the dominant species on the planet that we inhabit. 
 It can be argued that our competitive behaviour for resources is not yet fully dissolved. So there is always a prospect of an all-out nuclear war breaking out on our planet just by virtue of us being who we are. 
This can be our barrier which we then fail to pass and become extinct. 
- Interestingly there is also an hypothesis that states that we are already through the Great Filter and have evolved to a stage where we can potentially become a multi-galactic civilization. 
Based on the facts that we know about evolution, it is amazing to think that a bunch of 'dead chemistry' evolved to become who we are today. It can get so complex that is at times beyond magic. 
Everytime I learn something about our human biology, be it the brain or the body, I am thorougly amazed and perplexed at the complexity of our systems.
- Yet some scientists argue that on any planet where conditions are suitable for evolution, evolution is a very natural and almost unabating process. 
- Well I guess we will have to wait and watch which one of them is true and if anyone of them is true at all. As I like to believe, we still have a long way to go before we can understand thoroughly the complexity of our universe and finally get closer to the answer to the meaning of life( and no I don't think its 42).
